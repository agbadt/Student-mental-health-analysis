# Student Mental Health Analysis

## Overview
This project analyzes survey data from international students to explore how factors such as length of stay, social connectedness, and acculturative stress relate to mental health outcomes — specifically depression, anxiety, and loneliness.

## Objectives
- Identify which student groups are most at risk of poor mental health
- Explore the relationship between length of stay and mental health scores
- Use SQL to query and summarize key patterns in the data

## Tools & Skills Used
- SQL (PostgreSQL)
- Data filtering, aggregation, and grouping
- Exploratory data analysis

## Key Findings
- International students who had stayed longer showed varying levels of depression and social connectedness
- Students with lower social connectedness scores tended to report higher depression scores
- The data highlights the importance of early mental health support for international students

## Dataset
Data sourced from a study on international student mental health, made available via DataCamp.

## About
This project was completed as part of the DataCamp Data Analyst curriculum.
